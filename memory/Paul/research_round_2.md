PRE-DEBATE RESEARCH NOTES - AI ERA EMPLOYMENT
Position: Market Innovation & Entrepreneurship Focus

KEY EVIDENCE:
• Historical precedent
- Industrial Revolution initially displaced workers but created new jobs
- 1990s internet revolution created more jobs than it eliminated
- 38% of US jobs in 2018 didn't exist in 1980 (WEF report)

• Current AI impact studies
- McKinsey: 30% of work hours automated by 2030
- PwC: 30% jobs at risk by mid-2030s
- MIT: AI creating more high-skilled jobs while automating routine tasks

POLICY PROPOSALS:
1. Entrepreneurship Support
- Tax incentives for startups
- Streamlined business registration
- Access to capital programs
- Regulatory sandboxes for innovation

2. Skills Transition
- Coding bootcamps
- Digital literacy programs
- Vocational retraining
- Micro-credentialing systems

3. Market Flexibility
- Gig economy framework
- Portable benefits
- Reduced barriers to self-employment
- Cross-border digital work permits

RISKS & CHALLENGES:
• Short-term displacement
- Skill mismatch
- Geographic concentration
- Age discrimination
- Income inequality spike

• Implementation hurdles
- Training costs
- Access disparities
- Policy resistance
- Market adaptation time

COUNTER-ARGUMENTS TO ADDRESS:
1. UBI advocacy
- Cost effectiveness concerns
- Work incentive impacts
- Market distortion risks

2. Government job guarantees
- Efficiency problems
- Innovation stifling
- Fiscal sustainability

SYNTHESIS:
Market-driven approaches offer most sustainable long-term solution but require:
1. Strong transition support
2. Rapid skill adaptation systems
3. Safety net modernization
4. Balanced regulation

Key argument: History shows markets create new opportunities faster than government intervention, but smart policy framework essential for smooth transition.

DEBATE TACTICS:
• Use historical examples
• Emphasize innovation potential
• Acknowledge transition challenges
• Focus on practical solutions
• Highlight individual agency
